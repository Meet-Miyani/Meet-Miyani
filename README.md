Five years building Android. The parts I find most interesting aren't the features — they're what's left behind: architecture that holds up, shared libraries that actually get used, codebases where the next developer can find their way around.

Day job is production Android at a food-tech company — three apps, one backbone library, a team that went from two to four. Side of that: personal Play Store apps since college, a couple of open-source projects, and some writing when something's worth explaining.

I like the deep end. KSP annotation processing, Compose Multiplatform, BLE hardware, bitmap-rendered POS receipts. When something is unfamiliar or barely documented, that's usually when the work gets interesting.
